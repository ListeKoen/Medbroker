Feasibility Prototypes

Concept and feasibility stages prove essential steps in design and development. Gathering information and completing tests during stages of concept and feasibility minimizes risk in product development and prevents unexpected test failures in design verification and validation testing.

Taking time early in design and development to explore the concept and ensure its feasibility can prevent costly delays due to product redesign and retesting.

After defining the purpose and requirement for the prototype, determination of fabrication method(s) follows. Criteria for fabrication method can include piece part and tooling cost/lead time, finish, dimensional accuracy, and strength of materials.

Usability

Medbroker helps companies understand how to institutionalise the usability process and to guide them through the usability standard. We also cunduct usability testing and reports for use as part of Technical file.

ISO/IEC 62366 is a process-based standard that aims to help manufacturers of medical devices ‘design in’ usability and 'design out’ use errors. The standard also applies to documentation that may accompany a device, and to the training of intended users.

Manufacturers that follow the standard must adopt a user-centred design process (called a Usability Engineering Process in the standard) that spans the design and development life cycle of the device. To comply, manufacturers must document the process in some detail in a Usability Engineering File.

Out reports can be used directly as part of Technical File for the product.
